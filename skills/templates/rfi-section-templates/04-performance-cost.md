## Purpose

This section requests specific, quantifiable information about technology performance and total cost of ownership. Modeled on the GSA RFI's Technology Performance section, it asks vendors to substantiate claims with data.

## Structure

### 4.1 Performance Metrics
- What is the primary performance metric for your technology? (e.g., % energy savings, response time, accuracy rate)
- What is the most common incumbent/alternative for your technology?
- Estimated performance improvement compared to the incumbent (%)
- What are the expected non-energy benefits? (limit 500 words)
- Provide third-party validation of performance claims (studies, certifications, test results)

### 4.2 Cost Information
- Expected upfront cost (by unit, door, sq ft, or building — specify the unit)
- Expected installation cost (by same unit)
- Expected ongoing costs (annual licensing, maintenance, support — by same unit)
- Expected payback period (years)
- Are there financing options? (upfront purchase, lease, subscription/SaaS, performance-based, gain-share)

### 4.3 Total Cost of Ownership
- Provide a 5-year total cost of ownership estimate for a building matching the project description in Section 1
- Include: hardware, software, installation, commissioning, training, annual licensing, maintenance, support
- Identify any costs that may vary (per-user fees, per-device fees, data storage costs)

### 4.4 Warranty & Support
- Product warranty periods (by component)
- Software support and update commitment (how long will software updates be provided?)
- Hardware end-of-life policy (what happens when hardware reaches end of life?)
- Support tiers and SLAs (response time, resolution time, availability hours)

### 4.5 Risk Factors
- What could prevent the technology from achieving stated performance levels?
- What building conditions or prerequisites must be met?
- What dependencies exist (network infrastructure, other systems, staffing)?

## Guidance for AI Generation

- Include relevant cost benchmarks from the market-statistics skill and technology-specific cost benchmarks so the owner can evaluate vendor pricing against market norms
- Request costs in units that match the building description (per sq ft for large buildings, per door for access control, etc.)
- Ask for 5-year TCO to surface ongoing costs that vendors may understate
- Include financing options — EaaS, Pay-As-You-Lease, and gain-share models are increasingly relevant
- Request warranty and end-of-life information to address the technology lifecycle mismatch problem
