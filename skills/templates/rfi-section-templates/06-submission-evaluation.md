## Purpose

This section tells vendors how to submit their response, what to include, and how responses will be evaluated. It establishes the ground rules for a fair, structured evaluation.

## Structure

### 6.1 Submission Instructions
- Submission deadline (date and time with timezone)
- Submission format (email, portal upload, physical delivery)
- Contact for questions (with deadline for questions)
- Page/word limits for responses
- Required file formats
- Number of copies (if applicable)

### 6.2 Required Submission Contents
- Completed response to all sections (1–5)
- Company overview / capability statement
- Architecture diagram
- Reference project list with contact information
- Pricing / cost information
- Product data sheets or technical specifications
- Relevant certifications and test results
- Single-page technology overview summary

### 6.3 Evaluation Criteria
Provide a weighted evaluation matrix so vendors understand priorities:

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Technical fit | X% | How well the technology addresses stated use cases and outcomes |
| Integration capability | X% | Interoperability with existing systems, open standards support |
| Vendor viability | X% | Financial stability, market presence, track record |
| Total cost of ownership | X% | 5-year TCO including all hardware, software, support, and operational costs |
| Cybersecurity posture | X% | Certifications, patch management, incident response capability |
| Implementation approach | X% | Deployment timeline, commissioning plan, training, ongoing support |
| Innovation & roadmap | X% | Future capabilities, technology evolution, backward compatibility |

### 6.4 Evaluation Process
- How responses will be reviewed (internal team, MSI consultant, scoring panel)
- Whether shortlisted vendors will be invited for demonstrations or site visits
- Timeline for evaluation and notification of results
- Whether this RFI may lead to a formal RFP process

### 6.5 Terms & Conditions
- Statement that the RFI does not constitute a commitment to purchase
- Confidentiality provisions (how vendor responses will be handled)
- No reimbursement for preparation costs
- Right to cancel the process
- Intellectual property: who owns any ideas or approaches submitted

## Guidance for AI Generation

- Weight evaluation criteria based on the user's priorities from the canvas walkthrough (e.g., if cybersecurity was emphasized in Section 4, weight it higher)
- Adjust submission requirements to match the scope — a small office project needs less formal submissions than a major hospital
- Include realistic timelines (vendors typically need 3–4 weeks to prepare quality RFI responses)
- Note that weighted criteria should total 100% and reflect the owner's actual priorities
