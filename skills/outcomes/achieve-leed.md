## Overview

LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is the most widely recognized green building certification globally, administered by the US Green Building Council (USGBC). LEED certification increases rental premiums by 8.5–25% and signals to tenants, investors, and regulators that a building meets rigorous environmental performance standards.

LEED operates on a points system with four certification levels:
- **Certified:** 40–49 points
- **Silver:** 50–59 points
- **Gold:** 60–79 points
- **Platinum:** 80+ points

Smart building technology contributes to LEED credits across multiple categories, particularly Energy & Atmosphere, Indoor Environmental Quality, and Innovation.

## LEED Categories Where Technology Contributes

### Energy & Atmosphere (up to 33 points)
This is where smart building technology has the most direct impact:
- **Optimize Energy Performance** (up to 18 points) — BMS optimization, HVAC controls, lighting controls, sub-metering. The more energy you save beyond the ASHRAE 90.1 baseline, the more points.
- **Advanced Energy Metering** (1 point) — Sub-metering of all energy sources at the building and major system level.
- **Demand Response** (2 points) — Infrastructure to participate in demand response programs.
- **Renewable Energy** (up to 5 points) — On-site renewable generation.
- **Enhanced Commissioning** (up to 6 points) — Monitoring-based commissioning (MBCx) using BMS data.

### Indoor Environmental Quality (up to 16 points)
- **Indoor Air Quality Monitoring** (1 point) — CO2 monitoring in densely occupied spaces + outdoor air monitoring.
- **Thermal Comfort Monitoring** (1 point) — Permanent temperature/humidity monitoring + occupant survey.
- **Interior Lighting** (2 points) — Individual lighting controls, occupancy sensors, daylight-responsive controls.
- **Daylight** (up to 3 points) — Automated shading systems can help optimize daylight while preventing glare.

### Innovation (up to 6 points)
- Smart building features that go beyond standard LEED requirements can earn Innovation credits. Examples: real-time energy dashboards, occupant engagement platforms, advanced fault detection.

## Technology Priorities for LEED

1. **BMS/BAS with advanced sequencing** — Foundation for energy optimization credits. Must support ASHRAE 90.1 compliance documentation and ongoing performance monitoring.
2. **Sub-metering / EMIS** — Required for Advanced Energy Metering credit. Enables Optimize Energy Performance documentation through continuous monitoring.
3. **IAQ monitoring** — CO2 sensors in all densely occupied spaces, outdoor air monitoring station. Supports Indoor Air Quality Assessment credits.
4. **Lighting controls** — Occupancy sensors, daylight-responsive dimming, individual controls. Contributes to both energy and IEQ credits.
5. **Commissioning support** — Technology that enables monitoring-based commissioning (MBCx) for Enhanced Commissioning credits.

## Related Certifications

| Certification | Focus | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **WELL Building Standard** | Occupant health and wellness | Complements LEED; significant technology overlap in IAQ, thermal comfort, lighting |
| **SmartScore** (WiredScore) | Technology capability + user functionality | Focuses specifically on smart building features; launched 2021 |
| **BREEAM** | Environmental performance (UK/Europe) | Similar to LEED but more prevalent outside North America |
| **ENERGY STAR** | Energy performance benchmarking | ENERGY STAR score used as input for LEED energy credits |
| **NABERS** | Building energy rating (Australia) | National Australian rating system |
| **Green Star** | Sustainability (Australia/NZ) | Regional equivalent of LEED |

## Canvas Section Guidance

### Section 1: Goals
Be specific about the target LEED level (Gold vs. Platinum) because it significantly affects technology scope and budget. LEED Gold is achievable with good design and standard smart building tech; Platinum requires innovation and advanced optimization.

### Section 2: Actions
Engage a LEED consultant early (RIBA Stage 2) to map specific credits to technology requirements. Not all LEED points require technology — site selection, materials, and water efficiency are separate categories.

### Section 4: Costs & Risk
LEED certification has direct costs (registration, certification fees, consultant fees) and indirect costs (technology and design decisions driven by credit requirements). Budget 1–3% of construction cost for LEED-related incremental costs on new builds.

### Section 7: KPIs
- LEED points achieved (target vs. actual)
- ENERGY STAR score (prerequisite: minimum 75 for energy credits)
- Energy Use Intensity (EUI) vs. ASHRAE 90.1 baseline
- IAQ metrics (CO2 levels, outdoor air delivery rates)
- Post-occupancy survey results (thermal comfort, lighting satisfaction)
