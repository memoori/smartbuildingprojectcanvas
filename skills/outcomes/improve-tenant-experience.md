## Overview

Tenant experience is the primary competitive differentiator in commercial real estate. 91% of tenants say they are willing to pay a premium for tech-enhanced workspaces, and 75% of CRE and retail executives consider smart buildings essential (Forrester/Johnson Controls, 2024). LEED-certified buildings command rental premiums of 8.5–25%.

In the context of smart buildings, tenant experience encompasses comfort (thermal, air quality, lighting), convenience (access, wayfinding, booking), connectivity (reliable, fast networking), and responsiveness (maintenance requests handled quickly, building adapts to how people use it).

The post-pandemic shift to hybrid work has raised the bar: when employees can work from home, the building must offer a meaningfully better experience to justify the commute.

## What Tenants Actually Want

Research consistently shows tenants prioritize:

1. **Thermal comfort** — The #1 complaint in commercial buildings. People want comfortable temperatures and the perception of control (personal comfort apps, operable thermostats).
2. **Air quality** — Post-COVID awareness has made indoor air quality visible. Tenants want to see that ventilation is adequate, CO2 levels are monitored, and filtration is sufficient.
3. **Frictionless access** — Mobile credentials, touchless entry, seamless visitor management. Nobody wants to carry a badge in 2026.
4. **Reliable connectivity** — Fast, reliable Wi-Fi and cellular coverage throughout the building. This is table stakes, not a differentiator.
5. **Responsive maintenance** — When something breaks, the building should know before the tenant reports it (predictive maintenance), and fix it quickly.
6. **Flexible space** — Easy room and desk booking, real-time availability, the ability to find and use different spaces throughout the day.

## Technology Priorities for This Outcome

1. **Tenant experience / workplace apps** — Mobile apps for access, room/desk booking, comfort adjustment, service requests, wayfinding, amenity booking.
2. **Access control modernization** — Mobile credentials, visitor management, touchless entry. The lobby experience sets the tone.
3. **Indoor environmental quality monitoring** — Real-time air quality displays (CO2, PM2.5, humidity) build trust and support WELL/SmartScore certification.
4. **Occupancy sensing + space analytics** — Real-time availability of desks, rooms, and amenity spaces. Data-driven decisions on space configuration.
5. **HVAC optimization** — Responsive comfort that adapts to actual conditions and occupant feedback, not just fixed schedules.

## The ROI Argument

Tenant experience improvements are harder to quantify than energy savings, but the business case is strong:

- **Tenant retention:** Replacing a tenant costs 12–18 months of rent (downtime, TI, commissions). Improving retention by even one lease renewal per year can justify significant technology investment.
- **Rental premium:** LEED-certified buildings command 8.5–25% higher rents. SmartScore and WELL add further premium.
- **Occupancy rates:** Tech-enabled buildings maintain higher occupancy in soft markets.
- **Talent attraction:** Tenants choose buildings that help them attract and retain employees. The building is part of the employer brand.

## Canvas Section Guidance

### Section 1: Goals
Identify specific tenant pain points before selecting technology. Survey current tenants: What do they complain about? What do they wish the building did? The answer might be "fix the elevators" not "install an AI platform."

### Section 2: Actions
Start with high-visibility, low-complexity improvements: lobby experience, mobile access, responsive maintenance. Build credibility before deploying more complex systems.

### Section 7: KPIs
- Tenant satisfaction score (annual survey, standardized questions)
- Tenant retention rate (renewals / expiring leases)
- Net Promoter Score (would tenants recommend the building?)
- Maintenance request response time
- Indoor air quality metrics (CO2 average, % of hours within comfort band)
- Thermal comfort complaints per 1,000 occupied sq ft
- Space utilization rate (if relevant to hybrid work optimization)
