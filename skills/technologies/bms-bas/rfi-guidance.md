## Questions to Ask Vendors

### Technology Overview
- Describe your BMS/BAS platform architecture (edge, cloud, hybrid). What runs locally vs. in the cloud?
- Which communication protocols does your system natively support (BACnet, Modbus, LonWorks, KNX, MQTT)?
- Does your platform expose open APIs (REST, GraphQL) for integration with third-party systems? Provide API documentation.
- How does your system handle semantic data modeling? Do you support Project Haystack, Brick Schema, or ASHRAE 223P tagging?
- What is your approach to legacy system integration? Can your platform overlay onto existing BAS without rip-and-replace?

### Functionality & Use Cases
- Describe your fault detection and diagnostics (FDD) capabilities. Is FDD rule-based, ML-powered, or both?
- What occupancy-responsive control strategies does your platform support?
- How does your system handle demand-controlled ventilation?
- Describe your alarming and notification capabilities. Can alarm rules be customized per zone, schedule, and severity?
- What portfolio-level analytics and benchmarking capabilities do you offer?

### Integration & Interoperability
- How does your BMS integrate with access control, lighting, fire/life safety, and vertical transport systems?
- Can your platform act as a Master Systems Integrator layer, normalizing data from multiple vendor subsystems?
- What data export formats do you support? Can building owners extract their full operational dataset at any time?
- Describe your approach to vendor lock-in mitigation. What happens to our data and operations if we switch providers?

### Cybersecurity
- What cybersecurity certifications does your platform hold (SOC 2, ISO 27001, IEC 62443)?
- How do you handle IT/OT network segmentation?
- Describe your patch management and firmware update process. How frequently are security patches released?
- What access control and audit logging capabilities does your platform provide?

### Deployment & Support
- What are the physical infrastructure requirements (network, server, power) for your platform?
- Describe your commissioning process. What documentation and training do you provide?
- What are your support SLAs (response time, resolution time, availability)?
- Describe your product roadmap for the next 24 months.

## What Vendor Responses Should Include

- Platform architecture diagram showing edge/cloud components
- List of natively supported protocols with version numbers
- API documentation or links to developer portal
- At least 3 reference projects in comparable building types with contact information
- Cybersecurity certifications and penetration test results (redacted)
- Total cost of ownership breakdown (hardware, software, installation, annual licensing, support)
- Data portability statement — how the owner can export or migrate their data

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | What to Look For |
|---|---|---|
| Interoperability | High | Native multi-protocol support, open APIs, semantic data modeling |
| Cybersecurity | High | Certifications, patch cadence, IT/OT segmentation approach |
| Total cost of ownership | High | Transparent pricing, OPEX vs CAPEX options, no hidden fees |
| Scalability | Medium | Portfolio management, multi-site support, cloud architecture |
| Vendor viability | Medium | Financial stability, market presence, product roadmap |
| Ease of use | Medium | Operator interface, training requirements, mobile access |
| Analytics & AI | Medium | FDD capabilities, energy optimization, predictive maintenance |
| Data ownership | High | Clear contractual data ownership, export capabilities, no lock-in |

## Red Flags & Common Pitfalls

- **Proprietary protocols only** — If the vendor cannot communicate via BACnet or other open protocols, you risk complete vendor lock-in
- **No API access** — Modern BMS platforms must expose APIs for integration. "We handle all integrations" is a lock-in strategy
- **Vague cybersecurity answers** — If the vendor cannot name specific certifications or describe their patch process, their security posture is likely immature
- **No reference projects in your building type** — A vendor strong in offices may be weak in healthcare or mixed-use
- **Bundled hardware + software with no separation** — Makes future migration extremely costly
- **No data export capability** — Your building operational data must be extractable in standard formats at all times

## Contract Clauses to Include

- **Data ownership:** All operational data generated by the BMS is owned by the building owner and must be exportable in open formats (CSV, JSON, BACnet)
- **Technology refresh provisions:** Contract must specify upgrade paths, backward compatibility commitments, and end-of-life timelines
- **Cybersecurity SLAs:** Maximum time to deploy critical security patches, breach notification timelines, penetration testing rights
- **Interoperability requirements:** Vendor must maintain open protocol support and API availability as a contractual obligation
- **Exit provisions:** Clear migration path and data portability if the owner switches vendors, including transition support period
